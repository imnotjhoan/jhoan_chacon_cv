Project Details

This was an engineering challenge proposed by the EIA university to design, build and evaluate a hovercraft capable of moving over smooth solid surfaces and water. The challenge also included the generation of energy from renewable sources as an essential part of the evaluation.

The beginning of the project was characterized by a process of research and state of the art, in order to understand the operation of these vehicles and to identify the essential elements for the design of the hovercraft, as well as for the generation of energy. It was identified that:

  • Air cushion design: Circular air cushions provide the most effective lift, but maneuverability is lost. Therefore, to improve forward motion, a rectangular design with rounded edges and an arc-shaped front end was considered optimal to reduce drag and increase stability. Avoiding sharp edges was essential to minimize turbulence and energy loss.
  • Propulsion and control: We were limited to using only two engines: one for lift and one for propulsion. This led to prioritizing the search for a lift engine based on the CFM (cubic feet per minute) it could provide, in order to ensure adequate airflow to keep the vehicle lifted.
  • Energy Generation: Several methods of energy harvesting were studied, including solar panels, wind turbines and piezoelectric materials. Using decision matrices to evaluate feasibility, efficiency and integration potential, the team selected solar panels as the most suitable option.
